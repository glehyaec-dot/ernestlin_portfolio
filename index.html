Scholarships and Awards
The Simon Bergman Memorial Scholarship (2024–2027)
A highly selective scholarship gifted by Fred and Leah Bergman in memory of Simon Bergman, awarded to a single Year 9 student each cycle. Valued at $4,000 disbursed across Years 10, 11 and 12, recognising students who demonstrate an affinity with Melbourne High School's ethos, outstanding academic progress, and active engagement across the broader curriculum. Successful applicants are selected for being forthright, imaginative, confident, motivated, well organised, and energetic members of their school community. Recipients are not eligible for other MHS scholarships in their remaining years.
The Webber Family Scholarship (2025)
An annual scholarship awarded to a single Year 9 or Year 10 student undertaking STEM-related subjects at Melbourne High School, demonstrating both excellent academic results and meaningful involvement in the broader aspects of student and community life. Established by Old Boy Greg Webber in the egalitarian spirit of Melbourne High School — honouring lifelong learning, growth, contribution, and a life of purpose. A financial hardship component may also be considered. Accompanied by a $1,000 payment and a formal certificate with citation.
Monash Scholars Program (2025–2027)
A selective university-run enrichment program now in its 14th year, admitting high-achieving Victorian secondary school students in Years 10 through 12. Scholars are invited to an exclusive program of on-campus events at Monash's metropolitan campuses aimed at building academic networks among like-minded peers, exploring Monash's ten faculties and diverse career pathways, and developing personal aspirations, workplace skills, and extra-curricular capabilities beyond the classroom. Scholars who proceed to Monash University after Year 12 receive continued leadership and ambassador opportunities.
