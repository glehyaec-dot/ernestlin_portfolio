Professional Summary

Hi, I'm Ernest, a high-achieving high school scholar and experienced youth leader, with career aspirations in clinical healthcare and medical research.

Academically, my interests in health, medical science and STEM motivate me to explore knowledge way beyond my years. Rather than the number of achievements or awards, I believe that personal growth defines my success. This drives me to tackle increasingly demanding challenges — from achieving top results internationally/nationally in Neuroscience, Science and Maths competitions, to conducting and exhibiting my own research in adolescent psychology. I'm grateful for all the opportunities and recognition I've had thus far, and will continue to push beyond my limits.

My greatest passion of all is volunteering. "The purpose of life is to serve" — through early exposure to community service, the privilege of making a direct impact on others quickly grew into something I loved. I currently serve in multiple concurrent roles that deliver projects and initiatives in health, education and social equity. I work with organizations including International Leo Club, SWAN Australia and Royal Children's Hospital, and engage in roles spanning frontline volunteering, team leadership, advisory work, research, event organization and more. I'm especially passionate about serving vulnerable groups, such as rare disease patients, intellectually disabled students, and underprivileged families. It is through direct interactions with those in need — understanding their difficulties, hearing their stories, and being there for them as a helpful hand or affectionate smile — that sparked my aspirations to work in health.

At Melbourne High School, I devote time outside of academic studies to various leadership roles. Whether teaching passionate students advanced Maths topics or representing and advocating for students' wellbeing, I enjoy using my expertise and skills to better students' experiences at school. Through these commitments, I've also developed crucial leadership, interpersonal and career skills, such as public speaking, formal communication, project/team management, etc. I feel privileged to be recognised by multiple scholarships and school merits for my dedication to broader student life.

Working towards my career in healthcare, I've been proactively pursuing opportunities across a diversity of health-related fields, completing student internships in medical research, public/private hospitals, private clinic, community pharmacy and other settings. I'm particularly interested in observing how healthcare professionals communicate with patients and collaborate with other healthcare roles. Through my job as a Pharmacist Assistant, I've gained real-life experience being part of the process of providing medical care, while also observing community pharmacists in their roles. Additionally, I've completed various qualifications and certificates in clinical practice, hospital volunteering, emergency care, psychology, medical research and public health, equipping myself for all kinds of experiences in the future.

Outside of school and leadership, I stay heavily involved in sports and music, which have been sustained commitments throughout my childhood. It's an incredible joy to achieve State Champion in table tennis — a sport I train for hours each week and have a great love for.

As I near the end of my high school studies, I am focused on working towards a career that combines clinical medicine/pharmacy, research and development, as well as patient advocacy, with particular focuses on rare diseases, neuroscience, and personalised drug therapies.
